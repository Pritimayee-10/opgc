Coal Handling Plant

The Coal Handling Plant (CHP) at OPGC is a critical facility responsible for receiving, storing, and supplying coal to the boiler units in a continuous and controlled manner. It ensures the efficient handling of raw coalâ€”from unloading at the railway yard or mine end to its final crushing, screening, and feeding into the boiler furnace. The CHP includes components such as track hoppers, conveyor belts, crushers, stacker-cum-reclaimers, and magnetic separators to facilitate smooth and automated coal flow, maintaining consistent fuel supply for uninterrupted power generation.

Main Systems Involved

  • Unloading: Wagon Tippler and Track Hopper receive coal from rail wagons.
  • Conveying: Belt conveyors transport coal across storage and feeding units.
  • Crushing: Crushers reduce large lumps to feedable size before entering the boiler.
  • Stacking & Reclaiming: Stacker Reclaimer stores and feeds coal into the system based on demand.

Coal Delivery

Coal is delivered to power plants based on their geographical location. For plants near rivers or coastal areas, coal is transported via ships or barges. In inland locations like OPGC, coal is primarily supplied through railway wagons or, where rail infrastructure is absent, by road through trucks. The choice of delivery mode depends on availability, distance, and logistics cost.

Coal Unloading

The method of unloading coal depends on the delivery vehicle. If trucks are used, they can directly dump coal into outdoor storage yards without the need for unloading equipment. However, when coal is brought by railway wagons or ships, mechanical systems such as car shakers, rotary car dumpers, grab buckets, cranes, and coal accelerators are used to unload the material efficiently and safely.

Coal Preparation

Raw coal often arrives in large lumps that are not suitable for combustion. To ensure efficient burning in boilers, coal undergoes a preparation process. This includes crushing, breaking, and sizing the coal using crushers, breakers, and sizers. Dryers may be used to remove moisture, and magnetic separators help remove any metallic impurities.

Coal Transfer

Once prepared, coal is transported to dead storage areas using a variety of mechanical systems. Commonly used equipment includes belt conveyors, screw conveyors, bucket elevators, and grab bucket elevators. These systems ensure continuous and controlled movement of coal within the plant premises.
